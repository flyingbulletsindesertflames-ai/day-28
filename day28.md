Today's Task: Hospital Admission Readiness Simulator
Hospital admissions aren't just a clinical decision — they're a coordination problem. A patient can be medically ready and still get stuck because prior authorization is pending, a bed isn't assigned, or consent paperwork is incomplete. Today's build turned that operational complexity into an interactive simulation using Claude: a single-page app where you play Hospital Admission Coordinator, working through PA status, insurance verification, documentation, physician orders, consent, and bed assignment — all weighted into a live readiness score that decides whether a patient is actually ready to be admitted.
What this exercise covers:

Admission Readiness — the operational checklist that has to clear before a patient is formally admitted, beyond just "the doctor said yes."
Healthcare Operations — how documentation gaps, insurance verification, and prior authorization status directly gate admission, not just billing.
Risk Management — spotting and resolving administrative risk (paperwork, payer issues) and clinical risk (medical necessity criteria) before they become denials.
Interactive Simulation — using Claude to go from a written spec straight to a working browser-based app with state logic, branching workflows, and scoring — not just a mockup.
